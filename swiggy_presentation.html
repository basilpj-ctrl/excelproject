01 — METHODOLOGY
WHAT WAS BUILT

The raw Swiggy dataset was structured into an Excel workbook with four layers — raw data, pivot-based analysis, KPI calculations, and an interactive dashboard.

🗃️
Data Sheet
Raw transactional data covering orders, dish names, food type (Veg/Non-Veg), city, state, price, ratings, and date. This was the source of truth for all analysis.
🔄
Pivot Tables
Multiple pivot tables were built on the Analysis Sheet — Monthly Trend, Daily Trend, Food Type split, Quarterly Summary, State-wise breakdown, and Top 5 Cities.
📐
KPI Calculations
Five KPIs were computed: Total Sales (SUM), Average Rating (AVERAGE), Rating Count, Order Count (COUNT), and AOV (Average Order Value = Sales ÷ Orders).
📈
Charts
Line chart for monthly trends, bar charts for daily and weekly patterns, donut chart for food type split, horizontal bars for city ranking, and a Bing map for state heatmap.
🎛️
Interactive Dashboard
A single-page Excel dashboard with slicers (Month, Category, Restaurant) so stakeholders can filter all visuals dynamically without touching raw data.
🗺️
Geo Heatmap
A filled map of India (via Excel's Bing Maps) visualizes state-level revenue — darker shades indicate higher sales — giving instant geographic context.

06 — SO WHAT?
WHAT THE DATA RECOMMENDS

The analysis isn't just descriptive — it points to clear action areas for growth.

01
🗓️ Demand
Fix the Tuesday Slump
Tuesday is consistently the lowest-revenue day. A weekday deal ("Tue Specials") could shift demand and improve restaurant kitchen utilization throughout the week.
02
🥩 Category
Grow the Non-Veg Segment
Non-veg is 35% of revenue — significant but underdeveloped. Dedicated campaigns for biryani, kebabs, and seafood could unlock a high-AOV growth lane.
03
🏙️ Geography
Activate Tier-2 Cities
States like Assam, Bihar, and Chhattisgarh sit at ~₹1.2M each — far below their potential. Lower competition there means cheaper growth vs. saturated metros.
04
📅 Seasonal
Capitalize on February
Every February shows a dip. Plan proactively: Valentine's Day meal combos, restaurant spotlights, and loyalty double-points could recover ₹200–300K in lost revenue.
05
⭐ Retention
Protect the 4.34 Rating
At 5.59M reviews, the rating is a brand moat. Any service degradation at scale is hard to recover from. This KPI deserves dashboard priority attention every week.
06
🎯 Risk
Reduce Bengaluru Dependency
One city driving ~10% of all revenue is a concentration risk. If Bengaluru faces a market disruption (competition, pricing), overall numbers take a significant hit.
